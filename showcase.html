03 // SHAP Feature Impact

Which Signals Drive Pain Predictions

TreeSHAP applied to both RF models across all 67 LOSO folds (2,680 samples). Mean |SHAP value| = average absolute contribution to the PA3 prediction decision.

Biosignal Features · GSR / ECG / EMG / HRV

Biosignal Key Insight

GSR SLOPE is the #1 driver — sweat gland activation speed is 3x more important than the next feature. Skin conductance responds faster than heart or muscle to heat pain, making it the most sensitive real-time marker. The nonlinear threshold response is exactly why Random Forest outperforms linear models here.

Facial Landmark Features · MediaPipe FaceMesh · 468 points
Feature Glossary
FeatureSHAPClinical Meaning
Landmark Key Insight

All top landmark features end in _std (variability). PA3 pain is not about where the mouth or brows are positioned — it is about how much they fluctuate across the 5.5-second window. Pain expression is dynamic, not static.

Combined Top-16 Across Both Modalities

Biosignal and landmark features are interleaved — both modalities carry non-redundant information about pain. Neither alone is sufficient.

Why Random Forest over Deep Learning?

With 67 subjects and ~2,680 samples, deep models overfit catastrophically in LOSO. TCN achieved 55.9%, MLP 51.2%, R3D-18 64.1%. Foundation models BIOT (54.4%), PainFormer (53.1%), Tiny-BioMoE (56.7%) all underperformed. RF is the empirically correct choice for this dataset size.

Why Stacked Fusion over Early Fusion?

Early fusion (concatenate all 57 features into one RF): 64.6%. Stacked fusion (RF-bio + RF-lm → LogReg meta-learner): 65.3%. The gain comes from each modality producing calibrated probabilities that the meta-learner combines — rather than raw features on incompatible scales.

SHAP Beeswarm · How to Read It
Biosignal Beeswarm

Each dot = one recording. Horizontal position shows whether that feature pushed toward PA2 (left) or PA3 (right). Color shows the raw feature value — blue = low, red = high.

For gsr_slope: the wide horizontal spread shows it has the most influence. The nonlinear pattern (both colors appearing on both sides) is exactly why Random Forest beats linear models — the relationship is threshold-based, not linear.

Landmark Beeswarm

Same interpretation. For mouth_height_std: samples with high mouth variability (red = large _std values) cluster toward PA3 — the patient is opening and closing their mouth more during the window.

Low variability (blue = still mouth) clusters toward PA2. This confirms why _std features dominate — pain is expressed through movement dynamics, not a fixed facial position.

Why the Variance?

Some people's bodies respond almost identically to 43°C and 45°C. Their sweat, heart, and facial expression show nearly the same pattern at both temperatures. Even a human expert would struggle. The ±14.1% std dev is a property of biology, not the algorithm.

Accuracy Distribution · 67 Subjects
Why LOSO and Not Random Split?

Random splits allow the model to see samples from the same person in both training and test — it can memorize individual physiology (e.g. "high GSR baseline = PA3 for this person"). LOSO forces the model to generalize to a completely new person never seen during training. That is the only evaluation relevant to clinical deployment.

Why Person-Specific Normalization?

Every person has a different physiological baseline. Person A's resting GSR might be 10 µS, Person B's might be 2 µS. Global normalization makes A's PA2 (12 µS) and B's PA3 (11 µS) look like the same pain level — but they are not.

Person-specific normalization converts each signal to "how far above your own baseline" — making individuals comparable across the 67-subject pool. This yielded +4% over global normalization (63.1% vs 59.1%).

06 // All Experiments

15+ Models — Chronological Ablation

Every model benchmarked on the same 67 reactive subjects using LOSO. Foundation models, temporal CNNs, graph networks, adversarial domain adaptation — all tried.

Why Foundation Models Underperformed

BIOT (54.4%), PainFormer (53.1%), Tiny-BioMoE (56.7%) were all pre-trained on large-scale biosignal corpora. But they were designed for long-duration signals (hours), not 5.5-second windows. Fine-tuning on 67 subjects is insufficient for their parameter counts. Domain mismatch + data scarcity = foundation models lose to RF here.

Why GNN + Biosignal Outperforms GNN Alone

GNN on raw landmarks alone: 51.7% — barely above chance. The raw (x,y) coordinate graph lacks the temporal summary statistics that matter. Fusing with biosignal RF probs: 63.1%. The biosignal carries the primary discriminative signal; GNN adds complementary spatial co-activation patterns from the face. Neither alone is sufficient.

* CrossMod-Transformer 2025 (87.5%) includes all 87 subjects — 20 non-reactive subjects with flat biosignals inflate results. Our LOSO-67 reactive-only protocol is stricter and clinically honest.
07 // How EmPath Works

Step-by-Step Pipeline

The Problem

Some patients — ICU sedation, infants, dementia patients — cannot tell you how much they hurt. Without self-reporting, clinicians must guess from behavior and physiology. EmPath automates this using sensors already present in many ICU environments.

The Task

Given a 5.5-second window of biosignals + facial video, is this person in moderate pain (PA2 ~43°C) or intense pain (PA3 ~45°C)? Only 1°C apart — one of the hardest pain discrimination tasks in the published literature.

01
Sensor Data
GSR · ECG · EMG
Three body sensors record for 5.5 seconds. GSR updates 512x/second, ECG reads heart rhythm, EMG reads shoulder/neck muscle tension.
02
Facial Video
25 fps camera
24 frames extracted across the window. MediaPipe FaceMesh maps 468 precise landmarks — every brow, eye, nose, and mouth point.
03
Feature Extraction
57 total features
35 statistical + HRV features from biosignals. 22 geometric measurements from the face (distances, ratios, variability). No raw pixels.
04
Two RF Models
300 trees each
RF(biosignal) and RF(landmark) are trained independently. Each outputs PA2/PA3 probabilities without seeing the other modality.
05
Meta-Learner
Logistic Regression
Receives 4 probability values from both RFs. Learns the optimal linear combination. This is stacking — a proven two-level ensemble technique.
06
SHAP Explanation
TreeExplainer
Every prediction has a feature-level justification. SHAP values show exactly which signals drove the PA2 or PA3 decision for each sample.
